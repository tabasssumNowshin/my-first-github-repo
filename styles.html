COX'SBAZAR

Cox's Bazar, located in southeastern Bangladesh, is a renowned coastal town famous for its long sandy beach, which stretches uninterrupted for about 120 kilometers, making it the longest natural sea beach in the world. This picturesque destination is bordered by the turquoise waters of the Bay of Bengal and lush green hills, creating a stunning backdrop that attracts both local and international tourists. The town is named after Captain Hiram Cox, a British officer who played a significant role in rehabilitating Arakanese refugees in the area during the late 18th century. Besides its breathtaking beaches, Cox's Bazar is also known for its vibrant fishing culture, the colorful markets selling seashells and local handicrafts, and its rich biodiversity, which includes the Himchari National Park and the nearby Teknaf Peninsula. It serves as a gateway to other attractions such as Saint Martin's Island, the only coral island in Bangladesh, and the majestic waterfalls and tribal communities in Bandarban. Cox's Bazar is not just a hub for relaxation but also a focal point for adventure enthusiasts and nature lovers.

BANDARBAN

Bandarban, a district in southeastern Bangladesh, is a mesmerizing hill town nestled in the Chittagong Hill Tracts, known for its breathtaking natural beauty, diverse cultures, and tranquil environment. It is surrounded by lush green hills, deep valleys, and winding rivers, making it one of the most scenic and serene destinations in the country.Some of the popular tourist attractions in Bandarban include:

  • Boga lake
  • Nilgiri hill
